## What Actually Makes It Different

A lot of distribution platforms pitch themselves as "artist-first" and then quietly take 9–15% of your royalties every time you get paid. At scale, that adds up to a pretty significant chunk of your career earnings going out the door indefinitely.

Unchained's model is simpler: you pay once a year, and that's it. Whether you release one track or fifty, whether you earn $100 or $100,000 in streams — the platform takes nothing from your royalties. The only exception is YouTube Content ID, which runs a 20% commission on YouTube-specific revenue, which is fairly standard across the industry.

The other thing that actually stands out is human review. Every single release that goes through Unchained gets manually checked before it hits the platforms. For an artist who's ever had a release yanked because of a metadata error, or had a remix credited wrong and triggered a copyright claim — this kind of gatekeeping is genuinely useful. It slows things down slightly compared to fully automated pipelines, but it catches problems before they become expensive headaches.

## The Services Beyond Distribution

Distribution is the foundation, but Unchained has built out a wider toolkit that most indie artists actually need:

**Playlist Pitching** — Third-party playlist pitching is included on both paid plans. Editorial pitching (Spotify's editorial team, basically) is a Pro-only feature. For artists trying to break into algorithm-driven discovery, this is a meaningful advantage.

**Royalty Advances** — If you've got streaming history, you can apply for an advance on projected future royalties. This is useful for artists who want to fund a video, a campaign, or pressing costs without giving up equity or signing anything long-term.

**AI Audio Mastering** — Available as a paid add-on on both plans. Useful if you're releasing frequently and don't want to pay per-track mastering rates every time.

**DIY Marketing / Digital Advertising** — The platform includes tools for automating social ads and running pre-save campaigns. More advanced release strategy, digital advertising management, and sync opportunities are available under the Pro tier on an application basis.

**Physical Distribution & Vinyl Pressing** — For artists who want to press records or distribute physically, Unchained handles that too. This is the kind of end-to-end infrastructure that used to require a label deal.

**Video Distribution** — Pro plan includes video distribution (to Vevo and similar), plus YouTube Content ID and official artist channel applications.

## The AI Music Policy (Actually Worth Reading)

In 2026, a distributor's position on AI-generated content matters more than most artists realize. As fully automated AI tracks flood streaming platforms and dilute royalty pools, who your distributor lets in directly affects your per-stream earnings.

Unchained has a published Statement on AI Music that takes a nuanced position: AI as a creative tool (for ideation, sound design, workflow) is permitted, but tracks created substantially or entirely by AI without meaningful human creative involvement get rejected. They also prohibit mass-produced generic content and royalty-farming submissions.

They're also active participants in the Principles for Music Creation with AI initiative alongside 100+ music tech companies. Whether you care about that kind of thing or not, it does mean the platform is actively managing what gets in — which protects everyone distributing legitimate music through it.
